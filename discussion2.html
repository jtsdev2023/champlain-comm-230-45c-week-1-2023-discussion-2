Discussion 2 - Small Group Communication and Skill

  • Strengths

    I rated myself high on the following items. In my mind, the main reason you wouldn't do well with one of these items is lack of confidence, fear of feeling stupid, fear of being wrong, and/or fear of criticism. After 18 years in the IT field, I for the most part have set these aside, and I no longer care if people/peers think I'm stupid or don't know what I'm doing.

    There are a few aspects of IT work that I have really come to appreciate:

    1. The breadth of technologies is vast, and, unless you're a savant and can do it all well, there is going to be things you don't know.
    2. There is unlimted potential to study and learn.
    3. There's always somebody smarter and that knows more.

    Strengths

    • Share my thoughts and ideas with the group
    • Share my feelings with the group
    • Disagree with other group members
    • Listen to the opposing ideas of other members
    • Listen to criticism from other members
    • Respond to criticism from other members
    • Consider points of view different from my own
    • Compromise when it will benefit the group
    • Facilitate negotiation within the group

  • Weaknesses/Areas in need of improvement
    • Small group participation

      My perception is that most of my small group participation is a negative experience due to bickering and infighting. As such, I generally tried to avoid it, if I can. But, avoiding small group participation in the information technology (IT) field is difficult. I'm hoping this course will help me become more proficient at small group communication and alleviate my aversion to small group participation.

    • Share my thoughts and ideas

      If the small group communication is not going well, I have a tendency to bottle up and only minimally participate. This is a weak area I would like to improve on, and I'm working through this course with that in mind.

    • Hubris

      Something not in exercise 1.2 that I struggle with in small group communication is hubris or thinking I know it all. Most of my small group communication is centered around technical items, and, after 18 years in the field, I have caught myself being arrogant and a "know it all", which is bad. So I actively monitor my behavior for self adjustment/correction.
